About Me

My name is Qingyu Meng, people know me also call me Beren.

As a dedicated MSc student in Statistics with a sharp mind for both academic and industrial needs, I thrive at the intersection of Statistical Modeling, Causal Representational Learning, Bayesian Methods, and Natural Language Processing. I also find many topics/techniques from the field of Data Architecture and MLOps quite useful. My commitment to lifelong learning fuels my expertise in Missing Data Imputation and Structural Equation Modeling, enhancing predictive accuracy and explainability. I possess a keen interest in developing Explainable NLP solutions, underpinned by a strong foundation in statistical methodologies and a passion for ethical AI. Eager to apply my skills in real-world challenges, I seek opportunities to contribute to innovative projects that prioritize transparency, data integrity, and align with business objectives.

In the near future, I plan to design this site as a blog hub for my thoughts/reflections with a (broad) focus on Missing Data Imputation, Synthetic Data Generation, Large Language Models, Causality, Bayesian Networks, and Data Architecture.
